Natural Hair Regrowth with PRP

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy is a cutting-edge, non-surgical treatment for hair loss that uses your own blood to promote hair growth. The process involves drawing a small sample of your blood, spinning it in a centrifuge to concentrate the platelets, and then injecting this "liquid gold" into the areas of your scalp with thinning hair.

These concentrated platelets are rich in growth factors that stimulate dormant hair follicles, increase circulation, and encourage the growth of new, healthier, and thicker hair. It is a safe and natural option for both men and women experiencing androgenetic alopecia (pattern baldness) or other forms of hair thinning.

The PRP Treatment Process

A simple, three-step process to rejuvenate your hair follicles.

1
Blood Draw

A small amount of blood is drawn from your arm, similar to a routine blood test.

2
Centrifuge Process

The blood sample is placed in a centrifuge, which spins at high speed to separate the platelet-rich plasma.

3
Scalp Injections

The concentrated PRP is carefully injected into targeted areas of the scalp to stimulate hair follicles.

PRP Therapy FAQs

Common questions about our PRP hair restoration treatments.

Patients typically experience minimal discomfort during the procedure. We can use a topical anesthetic or cooling device to ensure your comfort during the injections. Most clients describe the sensation as mild pinpricks that last only a few seconds.

We typically recommend an initial series of 3-4 treatments, spaced 4-6 weeks apart. Most patients begin to see initial results in hair texture and thickness within 2-3 months, with more significant improvement appearing after 6 months. Maintenance treatments are recommended every 6-12 months to sustain and enhance results.

Since PRP uses your own blood, there is virtually no risk of allergic reaction. You may experience mild redness, swelling, or tenderness at the injection sites, which typically resolves within 24-48 hours. There is no significant downtime, and you can resume most normal activities immediately. We recommend avoiding strenuous exercise and direct sun exposure for 24-48 hours post-treatment.

PRP is most effective for individuals in the early to moderate stages of hair loss who still have some active hair follicles. It works well for both men and women with androgenetic alopecia (pattern hair loss). During your consultation, we'll assess your specific condition and determine if PRP is the right solution for you. We may recommend combining PRP with other treatments for optimal results in more advanced cases.
